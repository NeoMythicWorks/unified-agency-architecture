Scope: Structural definition of measurement-grounded execution governance and externally enforced execution authorization.

1. Architectural Invariant

Unified Agency Architecture establishes a structural invariant: execution authority does not exist by default.

Execution authority exists only when admissibility conditions are measurably satisfied at execution time and authorization has been explicitly issued.

Execution capability and execution authority are structurally separated. Capability may be present within a system; authority must be conditionally derived from admissibility state.

2. Governance Model

Conventional governance mechanisms operate through policy declaration, role assignment, behavioral monitoring, or retrospective audit. These mechanisms influence behavior but do not determine whether execution authority exists at the moment execution occurs.

Unified Agency Architecture determines whether execution authority exists based on measured admissibility state.

Authorization issuance is conditional upon admissibility determination. If admissibility conditions are not satisfied, execution authority is not issued and execution cannot proceed.

3. Measurement and Admissibility

The architecture introduces a structural measurement layer that evaluates system conditions relevant to execution admissibility.

Measurement produces an admissibility state representing the evaluated condition of execution validity and boundary integrity.

Admissibility state is used solely to determine whether execution authority may exist. The internal construction of measurement functions, thresholds, and computational methods is implementation-defined and not specified in this document.

4. Authorization and Enforcement

Execution authority is externally issued and cryptographically verifiable.

Execution control points enforce structural dependency on valid authorization tokens. Execution without valid authorization is technically disallowed.

Authorization issuance is derived from admissibility state. Execution authority cannot exist independently of admissibility determination.

5. Execution Flow Model

Measurement ↓ Admissibility Determination ↓ Authorization Issuance ↓ Execution Control Point ↓ Execution Enabled or Blocked

6. Architecture Components

6.1 Measurement Layer

Evaluates system conditions relevant to execution admissibility and produces admissibility state.

6.2 Admissibility Determination Layer

Determines whether execution authority may exist based on admissibility state.

6.3 Authorization Issuance Layer

Issues cryptographically verifiable authorization tokens conditional upon admissibility determination.

6.4 Execution Control Layer

Enforces structural dependency between execution capability and valid authorization tokens.

7. Architectural Implication

Execution authority becomes conditionally issued rather than statically granted.

Governance becomes a measurable system condition rather than a policy declaration.

Execution authority cannot exist outside admissibility conditions.
